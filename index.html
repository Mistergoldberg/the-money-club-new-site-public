The Money Club.Org

A nonprofit teaching design thinking, how to build with AI, and financial literacy.

Build. Price. Sell.

The Money Club.Org is a startup-style summer program where kids learn how money moves by building real products through guided sprints. Every dollar is visible. Kids receive a $50 build budget to build, price, test and sell a product or service.

This is the model:

  • kids learn financial literacy through radical transparency
  • mentors get meaningful paid work
  • families help kickstart a local feedback loop of learning, demand, and community value

Our problem statement: We are trying to prove that a small, transparent nonprofit can create real value for kids and meaningful work for University of Toronto student mentors.

Build. Price. Sell.

Built for learning — not profit

Many kids already earn money in small ways—babysitting, tutoring, selling things.

The Money Club.Org takes that instinct further - it’s a startup-style program where kids learn how money moves by building real products through guided sprints.

Kids receive a $50 build budget to research ideas, create prototypes, test them, and learn what happens when an idea meets the real world.

That’s how they learn:

  • what makes something valuable
  • how pricing works
  • why some ideas hold up—and others don’t

This is financial literacy, design thinking, and AI—applied in practice.

Outcomes

Financial Literacy — How Money Works

Students learn:

  • Cost vs. price vs. margin
  • What makes something profitable
  • How pricing affects decisions

Design Thinking — Build What Works

Students learn how to think like designers

  • Test ideas with real people
  • Use feedback to improve
  • Understand what works and why

Build with AI

Students use AI throughout the program to research, prototype, test, and improve ideas faster.

  • Research markets
  • Analyze information and find opportunities
  • Model simple business cases
  • Create media and marketing
  • Build websites and app concepts

Who Runs It

Everyone deserves a mentor.
  • How to think about money.
  • How to spot value.
  • How to build something people actually want.
  • How to price it.
  • How to sell it.
  • How to recover when the first idea does not work.

The Money Club.Org is designed and led by Jared Goldberg — a product strategist and systems designer with 15+ years of experience building real-world business platforms.

The curriculum comes from the work itself: product development, retail, manufacturing, pricing, incentives, design thinking, and human behavior.

Jared has worked across Walmart, Loblaw, Canadian Tire, and China-based manufacturing ecosystems.

This July, he is running The Money Club as a focused one-week sprint: a practical, mentor-led program for students learning how ideas become real.
